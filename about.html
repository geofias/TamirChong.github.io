Research Experience

Undergraduate Thesis Project (2024–2025)
“Geochemical Nutrient Mapping of Agricultural Soils in Herrera (Panama Oeste) as a Tool for Farmers and the Sustainable Use of Natural Resources.”

This project focused on creating geochemical nutrient maps for agricultural soils in the Herrera township of Panamá Oeste. It involved:

  • Fieldwork: Collection of soil and water samples from agricultural sites.
  • Laboratory analysis: Determination of pH, electrical conductivity, soil texture, organic matter, and nutrient levels (nitrates, phosphates, etc.).
  • Geospatial data processing: Use of GIS and spatial interpolation methods to generate high-resolution nutrient distribution maps.
  • Applied outcomes: Development of a practical tool for farmers to support precision agriculture and sustainable soil management.

This research strengthened my expertise in geochemistry, spatial analysis, and environmental data science, while also training me to bridge scientific research with practical agricultural applications.
